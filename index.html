About Me

Hi, I’m Vamsi Sai Abhijit Tadepalli — a robotics engineer blending systems thinking with a bit of caffeine-fueled determination. I’m currently pursuing my M.S. in Robotics at ASU, where I specialize in computer vision, perception, and autonomy for intelligent robotic systems.

At the IRIS Lab, I contribute to vision-based tracking systems, currently refactoring and extending the TwinTrack framework for dynamic object tracking. My experience spans real-time control of drones for precision landings, and deep learning pipelines for industrial defect detection at BARC.

I enjoy working at the intersection of software and the physical world — building systems that not only run in simulation, but also survive it. My toolkit includes Python, C++, ROS2, OpenCV, and Linux, and my mindset is tuned for clean, modular, and testable code.

Project Highlights

TwinTrack:Vision-Based SLAM & Tracking Framework

Refactored and maintained TwinTrack’s codebase to improve clarity and support future research.

i. Maintained and refactored the official TwinTrack codebase — a dual-branch tracking system integrating short-term and long-term visual cues

ii. Added structured comments, cleaned legacy scripts, and resolved inconsistencies across modules for smoother execution

iii. Improved readability, modularity, and extensibility in preparation for integration with new visual recognition pipelines

Autonomous UAV for Target Tracking and Landing

Developed a Simulink model for the Parrot Mambo Minidrone to autonomously track and land on a moving target.

i. Tuned control parameters on the Parrot Mambo Minidrone to achieve stable flight with response times under 200 ms

ii. Applied adaptive color thresholding for reliable target detection under lighting variations of up to ±30% intensity

iii. Designed a sub-matrix vision algorithm enabling real-time tracking at 15 FPS and precision landings within ±10 cm of a moving target

Autonomous Mobile Manipulation using SLAM-Enabled TurtleBot

Implemented SLAM for autonomous navigation on a TurtleBot with LiDAR and a 4-DOF robotic arm for pick-and-place tasks.

i. Mapped ~40 m² environments with ±5 cm localization accuracy using 2D LiDAR SLAM

ii. Achieved >85% success over 20+ autonomous navigation trials with dynamic obstacle handling

iii. Executed pick-and-place tasks within a 1 m reach, averaging less that 1 min per cycle
